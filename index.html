G.A.I.N

G.A.I.N is an artificial Intelligence system backed by a trained model that can classify 10 types of food. Based on the food classification calories and points are associated. Points can be positive or negative depending on the food type one chooses and the balance of points after a yearly time frame ensures discounts to the health insurance premium.

Diet Chart

Diet Chart & Healthy Eating Tips

  • Idly (58 kcal/piece): Best consumed in the morning; pair with sambar for protein and fiber. Limit to 2–3 pieces per meal.
  • Masala Dosa (220 kcal): Good source of carbohydrates; opt for less oil. Have as breakfast or lunch, not dinner.
  • Pizza (285 kcal/slice): High in sodium and saturated fat; restrict to once a week. Choose thin-crust with vegetable toppings.
  • Jilabi / Jalebi (300 kcal): High sugar dessert; consume in very small portions. Avoid if diabetic or weight-managing.
  • Dhokla (160 kcal): Fermented, low-fat snack rich in protein. Suitable as an evening snack or light breakfast.
  • Dal Makhani (181 kcal/serving): Good protein and fiber source; limit butter/cream. Pair with whole-wheat roti.
  • Samosa (308 kcal): Deep-fried; high in calories. Limit to one piece occasionally; prefer baked versions.
  • Pakoda (287 kcal): Avoid frequent consumption. Reduce oil by using an air fryer.
  • Pani Puri (170 kcal): Watch portion size. Limit to 4–6 pieces.
  • Fried Rice (168 kcal/serving): Use less oil, add vegetables. Opt for brown rice for extra fiber.

ⓘ Daily calorie needs vary by age, gender and activity level. Aim for a balanced plate: 50% vegetables, 25% protein, 25% whole grains.
